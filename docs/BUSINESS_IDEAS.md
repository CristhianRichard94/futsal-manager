# Business Ideas

## Who pays
Charge venue owners, not players. Players won't pay for booking convenience (free alternatives: WhatsApp/call). Owners will pay to fill idle court hours and cut admin work.

## Revenue models
- SaaS fee per venue, tiered by # courts — predictable, easy to model.
- Take-rate on bookings (2-5%) — scales with venue success, low barrier to entry, needs payment processing built in.
- Freemium: free basic calendar + paid tier (analytics, no-show protection, marketing tools).
- Optional player-side convenience fee (priority booking, cancellation insurance) — small ARPU, skip unless growth stalls.

## Value to venue owner (core product)
- Dynamic pricing / last-minute discounts to kill empty slots.
- Deposit/card-hold on booking to cut no-shows.
- Auto-confirm + auto-reminders (WhatsApp/SMS).
- Recurring bookings for regular teams.
- Occupancy/peak-hour/revenue analytics dashboard.
- Multi-venue dashboard for owners running several courts.

## Value to player (retention, not revenue)
- Real-time availability aggregated across venues (find open court nearby).
- Team/group organizing: split payment, recurring weekly auto-booked games.
- Venue ratings/reviews + skill-matched pickup games (marketplace angle).

## Biggest differentiator
Don't be a single-venue booking calendar — be the city-wide aggregator across venues (OpenTable-for-futsal). Justifies %-of-booking fee since it's driving incremental demand, not just replacing a notebook.
